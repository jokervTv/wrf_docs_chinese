地图投影，科里奥利和曲率项

jokervTv 2020-01-14 translate WRF

ARW 求解器目前支持四种球体投影 —— 兰伯特正形投影，极地投影，墨卡托投影和经纬度投影。这些投影在 Haltiner 和 Williams（1980）中有所描述。对于其中的三个投影 —— 兰伯特正形投影，极地投影和墨卡托投影，变换是各向同性的。各向同性变换要求在网格的任何地方都要求 (Δx/Δy)∣earth=(\Delta x / \Delta y)|_{earth} =(Δx/Δy)∣earth​= 常数。 在以前的 ARW 版本中仅支持各向同性转换。 从 ARW 第 3 版开始，我们现在支持各向异性投影，在这种情况下为纬度-经度网格，并支持完整的纬度-经度全球模型。ARW 使用地图因子来实现投影，ARW V3 中引入的对各向异性变换的一般化要求从计算空间到物理空间的变换的 xxx 和 yyy 分量都必须有地图因子，以便适应各向异性。

The ARW solver currently supports four projections to the sphere— the Lambert conformal, polar stereographic, Mercator, and latitude-longitude projections. These projections are described in Haltiner and Williams (1980). The transformation is isotropic for three of these projections – the Lambert conformal, polar stereographic, and Mercator grids. An isotropic transformation requires (Δx/Δy)∣earth=constant(\Delta x / \Delta y)|_{earth} = constant(Δx/Δy)∣earth​=constant everywhere on the grid. Only isotropic transformations were supported in the previous ARW releases. Starting with the ARWV3 release, we now support anisotropic projections, in this case the latitude-longitude grid, and with it the full latitude-longitude global model. The ARW implements the projections using map factors, and the generalization to anisotropic transformations introduced in ARW V3 requires that there be map factors for both the xxx and yyy components of the transformation from computational to physical space in order to accommodate the anisotropy.

在 ARW 的计算空间中，Δx\Delta xΔx 和 Δy\Delta yΔy 是常数。球体的正交投影要求投影中网格点之间的物理距离随网格上的位置而变化。为了变换控制方程，将地图比例因子 mxm_xmx​ 和 mym_ymy​ 定义为计算空间中的距离与地球表面上相应距离的比值：

In the ARW’s computational space, Δx\Delta xΔx and Δy\Delta yΔy are constants. Orthogonal projections to the sphere require that the physical distances between grid points in the projection vary with position on the grid. To transform the governing equations, map scale factors mxm_xmx​ and mym_ymy​ are defined as the ratio of the distance in computational space to the corresponding distance on the earth’s surface:

(mx,my)=(Δx,Δy)DistanceOnTheEarth.(2.17)(m_x,m_y) = \frac{(\Delta x,\Delta y)}{DistanceOnTheEarth}. \tag{2.17} (mx​,my​)=DistanceOnTheEarth(Δx,Δy)​.(2.17)

The right-hand-side terms of the momentum equations (2.18) – (2.20) contain the Coriolis and curvature terms along with mixing terms and physical forcings. Including the map-scale factors (2.17), the Coriolis and curvature terms are cast in the following form:

where αr\alpha_rαr​ is the local rotation angle between the y-axis and the meridians, ψ\psiψ is the latitude, f=2Ωesin⁡ψf = 2\Omega_e\sin\psif=2Ωe​sinψ, e=2Ωecos⁡ψe = 2\Omega_e\cos\psie=2Ωe​cosψ, Ωe\Omega_eΩe​ is the angular rotation rate of the earth, and rer_ere​ is the radius of the earth. In this formulation we have approximated the radial distance from the center of the earth as the mean earth radius rer_ere​, and we have not taken into account the change in horizontal grid distance as a function of the radius. The terms containing the map-scale factors represent the horizontal curvature terms, those containing rer_ere​ relate to vertical (earth-surface) curvature, and those with eee and fff are the Coriolis force.

对于各向同性的投影（兰伯特正形投影，极地投影和墨卡托投影），地图比例因子在两个水平方向上都是相同的，因此 mx=my=mm_x = m_y = mmx​=my​=m，其中 mmm 通常仅随纬度变化。对于各向异性纬度-经度网格，mx=sec⁡ψm_x = \sec\psimx​=secψ 以及 my=1m_y = 1my​=1，假设 ∂/∂y=(1/re)∂/∂ψ\partial/\partial y = (1/r_e)\partial/\partial\psi∂/∂y=(1/re​)∂/∂ψ，则 ∂mx/∂y=(1/re)sec⁡ψtan⁡ψ\partial m_x/ \partial y = (1/r_e)\sec\psi\tan\psi∂mx​/∂y=(1/re​)secψtanψ 以及 ∂my/∂x=0\partial m_y/\partial x = 0∂my​/∂x=0。对于笛卡尔网格上的理想化情况，应指定地图比例因子 mx=my=1m_x = m_y = 1mx​=my​=1 ，指定 fff，并且 eee 和 re−1r^{-1}_ere−1​ 应为零以去除曲率项。

For the isotropic projections (Lambert conformal, polar stereographic, and Mercator), the map-scale factors are the same in both horizontal directions, such that mx=my=mm_x = m_y = mmx​=my​=m, where mmm typically only varies with latitude. For the anisotropic latitude-longitude grid, mx=sec⁡ψm_x = \sec\psimx​=secψ and my=1m_y = 1my​=1, so that ∂mx/∂y=(1/re)sec⁡ψtan⁡ψ\partial m_x/ \partial y = (1/r_e)\sec\psi\tan\psi∂mx​/∂y=(1/re​)secψtanψ and ∂my/∂x=0\partial m_y/\partial x = 0∂my​/∂x=0, given that ∂/∂y=(1/re)∂/∂ψ\partial/\partial y = (1/r_e)\partial/\partial\psi∂/∂y=(1/re​)∂/∂ψ. For idealized cases on a Cartesian grid, the map-scale factors mx=my=1m_x = m_y = 1mx​=my​=1, fff is specified, and eee and re−1r^{-1}_ere−1​ should be zero to remove the curvature terms.
